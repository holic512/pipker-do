46. Directions:
Translate the following text into Chinese. Write your translation on the ANSWER SHEET. (15 points)

You know the moment the conversation slows, then there's a pause. It's awkward, and so awkward that some people will panic and say anything. Do we all find such silences so stressful? Researchers analyzed the frequency and impact of gaps greater than 2 seconds during conversations, including an overview of previous studies which indicate that the fear of awkward silences can be so extreme that people avoid talking to strangers, even though doing so is likely to be an enjoyable experience.

In conversations with shorter gaps, people feel closer to their conversation partners, but if there are longer gaps, that sense of closeness clearly weakens. After a long gap, strangers are also more likely to change the topic. Yet the opposite seems to be true in conversations between friends. Longer gaps between friends appear to strengthen the relationship, allowing more time for reflection and expression.

### 范文

你知道那种时刻：对话慢下来，接着出现停顿。这很尴尬，尴尬到有些人会惊慌失措，随口说出些什么。我们是否都会觉得这样的沉默让人压力很大呢？研究人员分析了对话中超过两秒间隔出现的频率及其影响，并回顾了以往研究。这些研究表明，人们对尴尬沉默的恐惧有时会强烈到宁可避免与陌生人交谈，尽管这样做本来很可能会是一种愉快的体验。

在停顿较短的对话中，人们会觉得自己与谈话对象更加亲近；但如果停顿时间较长，这种亲近感就会明显减弱。长时间停顿之后，陌生人也更有可能转换话题。然而，在朋友之间的对话中，情况似乎正好相反。朋友之间较长的停顿反而会增强彼此关系，使人有更多时间进行思考和表达。
